📋 How to Play

Goal

Build a connected map of AP World History terms on the hexagonal board. Earn the most points by explaining how terms connect using historical thinking skills.

On Your Turn

  • Pick a tile from your hand and place it on the board — it must touch at least one tile already there.
  • Choose a thinking framework: Comparison, Causation, or CCOT.
  • Explain out loud how your tile connects to its neighbour using that framework.
  • Identify the SPICE-T theme your connection falls under.
  • For the highest score, add an appositive detail — a short phrase that gives extra historical context about one of the terms.

Group Vote

  • ✓✓✓ Excellent (+3 pts) — Strong explanation with a SPICE-T theme AND an appositive detail.
  • ✓ Good (+1 pt) — Valid explanation with a SPICE-T theme.
  • ✗ Challenge (0 pts) — Explanation needs more historical support.

Frameworks

  • Comparison — How are the two terms similar or different across societies?
  • Causation — How does one term cause or result from the other?
  • CCOT — How did something change or stay the same over time?

Hints

Tap a tile already on the board to see its SPICE-T themes for free. Tap a tile in your hand and choose Reveal Definition to see its full definition — this costs 1 point. In Solo Mode, hints are always free.
